## 1. Introduction and Background Facts
The homebuyers of a major housing project in Gurgaon challenged a unilateral clause in the builder-buyer agreement that allowed the developer to delay possession indefinitely. The developer had collected 93% of the flat cost and delayed construction by over 5 years. The RERA authority had directed a refund, which the builder appealed.

The dispute escalated when the adjudicating authorities passed their initial decrees, leading to appeals that eventually reached the highest appellate court. The case has significant implications for how commercial and real estate regulations are enforced across India.

## 2. Core Legal Issues Raised
Whether one-sided delay clauses in a builder-buyer contract are binding on flat purchasers, and the execution powers of RERA under Section 40.

In resolving these issues, the courts had to balance the protection of individual investors and homebuyers against the commercial flexibility of lenders and promoters.

### Arguments for the Respondents / State:
The builder argued that the buyers signed the contract voluntarily and are bound by the terms, which limit delay compensation to a nominal Rs 5 per sq ft per month. The homebuyers argued that they have zero bargaining power when purchasing flats and one-sided clauses are predatory and void under Indian contract principles.

The arguments centered heavily on the statutory interpretations of the relevant codes, and the legislative intent behind corporate insolvency and real estate consumer protection reforms in India.

## 4. Landmark Judgment and Detailed Analysis
The Supreme Court of India ruled that one-sided builder-buyer agreements are completely unfair and unenforceable. The Court held that RERA was specifically enacted to eliminate such asymmetric contracting. Under Section 18 of RERA, homebuyers are entitled to a full refund with interest at 10% per annum if the builder fails to deliver on time, overriding any contract terms.

The Supreme Court re-emphasized that the primary objective of modern Indian commercial law is to ensure transparency, accountability, and the speedy resolution of defaults.
